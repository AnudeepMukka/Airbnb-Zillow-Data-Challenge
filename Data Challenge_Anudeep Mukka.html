Introduction¶

Problem Statement¶

You are consulting for a real estate company that has a niche in purchasing properties to rent out short-term as part of their business model specifically within New York City. The real estate company has already concluded that two-bedroom properties are the most profitable; however, they do not know which zip codes are the best to invest in.

The real estate company has engaged your firm to build out a data product and provide your conclusions to help them understand which zip codes would generate the most profit on short term rentals within New York City.

You will be looking at publicly available data from Zillow and Airbnb:

  • Cost data: Zillow provides us an estimate of value for two-bedroom properties
  • Revenue data: Airbnb is the medium through which the investor plans to lease out their investment property. Fortunately for you, we are able to see how much properties in certain neighbourhoods rent out for in New York City

Assumptions¶

  • The investor will pay for the property in cash (i.e. no mortgage/interest rate will need to be accounted for)
  • The time value of money discount rate is 0% (i.e. $1 today is worth the same 100 years from now)
  • All properties and all square feet within each locale can be assumed to be homogeneous (i.e. a 1000 square foot property in a locale such as Bronx or Manhattan generates twice the revenue and costs twice as much as any other 500 square foot property within that same locale)
  • The occupancy rate is assumed to be 75%
  • Average yearly property growth are assumed constant. Average of year on year growth for the last 3 years is used to calculate growth and forecast property value for the next 5 years
  • We will be buying properties in '2017-06'
  • The yearly maintenance cost of the property is ~ 5% of the revenue generated by the property per year
  • Properties with square feet less than 80 are removed
  • Properties with a daily rental price greater than 2000 are removed

Product Overview¶

This data product takes Zillow dataset which contains historical property prices for various zip codes and Airbnb dataset which contains information about neighbourhood properties in various zipcodes as inputs. Quantitative metrics like revenue, profit, payback period, property growth and qualitative metrics like population size, number of super hosts have been used to recommend final zips. I have done all the analysis on 2 Bedroom properties in "New York" city. I have avoided hard-coding wherever possible and defined various functions which are driven by variables defined in "Input Variables" section to clean the data, derive new metrics and perform analysis on the data. This makes it reusable to solve similar business challenges.

Data Cleaning¶

Following data cleaning steps have been done:

  • Removed duplicates in listing dataset (1.06%)
  • Removed properties from listing dataset which has null zip values (1.05%)
  • Removed invalid zipcodes from listing dataset whose length is more than 5 (eg: '11249\n11249')
  • Removed properties with a daily rental price greater than 2000
  • Removed Properties with square feet less than 100
  • Removed special characters like '$' and ',' from price columns
  • Converted datatype of price to float and zipcodes to character
  • Properties in zip code '10013' which are mislisted under Brooklyn neighbourhood group have been changed to the Manhattan neighbourhood group

Property value for next 5 years¶

The Zillow dataset has past prices of the two-bedroom houses. In this section, I have tried to forecast the next 5 years of growth in the property prices based on the historic 3 years data. This is achieved by calculating the average year on year growth over the last 3 years and treating the average constant for the next 5 years. Also, since we are interested in 2 bedroom properties I have filtered the merged dataset for 2 bedroom properties

In [16]:
def avg_growth(x,start_year,end_year):
    Growth = []
    for i in range(start_year,end_year,1):
        past_year_month = str(i)+'-'+'06'
        present_year_month = str(i+1)+'-'+'06'
        Growth.append(((x[present_year_month]/x[past_year_month])-1))
   
    return np.mean(Growth)
In [17]:
def forecast(x,year):
    previous_year_month = str(year-1)+'-'+'06'
    return (1+x['Average_Growth'])*(x[previous_year_month])

Revenue, Profit and Break Even Calculations¶

Yearly revenue for a property is calculated using the occupancy rate and daily price of the property. I have considered 5% of the total revenue generated by a property goes towards maintenance costs and calculated yearly profit by taking off this cost from the revenue

In [21]:
def profit(x):
    Revenue_per_year = x['occupancy_rate'] * x['price'] *365
    Profit_per_year = (1-maintainance_cost)*Revenue_per_year
    return Profit_per_year
In [22]:
listing_merged['profit'] = listing_merged.apply(profit,axis = 1)

In which zipcodes majority of the superhosts invested?¶

Superhosts are experienced hosts who provide extraordinary experiences for their guests. The number of super hosts in a zip code is an important factor to be considered while purchasing a property as it is difficult to capture the market in zip codes where there is a large number of established super hosts. We can see that a larger number of super hosts are observed in Brooklyn borough and some high investment zip codes of Manhattan borough. One strategy is to choose zip codes which have less number of super hosts but offer higher returns. zipcodes such as 10025,11201,10011 offer higher returns and also have less number of super hosts

Recommendations¶

  • If yearly revenue or profit generated is criteria to decide on zip codes, properties in Manhattan should be bought. Zipcodes such as 10013,10014,10003 generate higher returns
  • If the payback period is criteria to decide on zip codes, properties in Staten Island, Queens should be bought. Zipcodes such as 10308,11434,10314 have a lower payback period
  • If higher property growth is criteria to decide on zip codes, properties in zip codes such as 10128,11217 and 10304 should be bought
  • If fewer super hosts and high returns are criteria to decide on zip codes, properties in zip codes such as 10025,11201,10011 should be bought
  • If popular zipcodes are criteria to decide on zipcodes, properties in zipcodes such as 11215,11217 and 10003 should be bought
